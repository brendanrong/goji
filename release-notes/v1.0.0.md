Goji 1.0. A redesigned settings window, a seamless notch HUD, and fixes for every way the app could freeze or fight the app you're typing in.

## What's new

- Settings, rebuilt: sidebar navigation (General, Microphone, Transcription, History, About) in a proper window that opens reliably on macOS 26.
- The notch HUD is now seamless, Willow style: exactly notch height with tight wings, nothing hanging below the menu bar, and the left wing shows the app you're dictating into.
- Compact right wing: a slim live waveform and a small amber mic glyph.

## Fixes

- Fixed a main-thread freeze (beachball) that could lock the app up after opening Settings or mid-dictation.
- Cancelling a recording with Esc no longer leaks the Esc key into the frontmost app, so it can't dismiss dialogs or stop things in the app you're dictating into.
- Pasting into Electron apps (Slack, Claude, and friends) is more reliable: the clipboard is restored after the paste has actually landed.
- Start and stop sound cues no longer drop when dictations come in fast.

## Install

1. Download Goji.dmg below.
2. Open it and drag Goji to your Applications folder.
3. Launch Goji. Grant Microphone (prompt) and Accessibility (System Settings > Privacy & Security > Accessibility).
4. On first launch it downloads the model once (about 600 MB). After that it works offline.

Requires macOS 14 or later. Apple Silicon recommended.
